Learn About Cybersecurity

On this page, you will learn about key areas of online safety, including strong passwords, phishing awareness, privacy management, social media safety, and device security.

Passwords

Passwords are your first line of defense against unauthorized access to your accounts. A strong password makes it much harder for attackers to gain access. It is recommended to use a mixture of lowercase and uppercase letters, numbers, and symbols. This increases the number of possible combinations, making it exponentially more difficult for a hacker to guess your password.

For example, a password with only lowercase letters might have 26 possible characters for each position. Adding uppercase letters doubles that to 52, adding numbers increases it to 62, and adding symbols can push it to over 90 characters per position. The more combinations there are, the longer it would take for someone to brute-force your password.

Tips for creating strong passwords:

  • Use at least 12 characters.
  • Include a combination of lowercase letters, uppercase letters, numbers, and symbols.
  • Avoid dictionary words or personal information such as names or birthdays.
  • Use a password manager to generate and securely store complex passwords.

Phishing

Privacy & Personal Information

Your personal information is valuable and should be protected online. Oversharing on social media or other platforms can expose you to identity theft, cyberbullying, and scams.

Tips for maintaining privacy online:

  • Limit the amount of personal information you share publicly.
  • Regularly review privacy settings on social media and apps.
  • Be cautious when filling out online forms, especially on unknown websites.
  • Consider using a nickname or alias instead of real names when possible.

Social Media Safety

Social media can be fun, but it can also expose you to risks. Protect your online identity and personal information by following simple safety practices.

Guidelines for safe social media usage:

  • Think carefully before posting anything public.
  • Use strong, unique passwords for each account.
  • Enable privacy settings to limit who can see your posts.
  • Be cautious with friend requests or messages from strangers.

Device Security

Keeping your devices secure is critical to protecting your data. Devices such as computers, phones, and tablets are often the target of malware and other cyberattacks.

Key practices for device security:

  • Keep your operating system and applications up to date.
  • Install reputable antivirus and anti-malware software.
  • Avoid downloading files or apps from untrusted sources.
  • Regularly back up important data to a secure location.
